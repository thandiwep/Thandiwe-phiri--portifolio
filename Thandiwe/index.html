About Thandiwe Phiri

As a 21-year-old Zambian woman and aspiring librarian and public administrator, I am passionate about empowering communities through access to information, education, and resources. My work is driven by a commitment to social justice, equity, and community well-being.

Librarian-related passions:

  • Information literacy: Empowering citizens to access and evaluate information effectively.
  • Knowledge sharing: Facilitating community access to information, education, and resources.
  • Collection development: Curating diverse, relevant materials for community needs.
  • Digital inclusion: Bridging the digital divide through library resources and training.
  • Intellectual freedom: Advocating for freedom of information and expression.

Public administration-related passions:

  • Policy development: Crafting policies promoting social justice, equity, and community well-being.
  • Community engagement: Fostering participatory governance and civic involvement.
  • Public service innovation: Implementing efficient, effective solutions for community problems.
  • Social justice: Addressing systemic inequalities through policy and programming.
  • Leadership development: Mentoring future public administrators and community leaders.

Intersections of librarianship and public administration:

  • Government information access: Ensuring public access to government documents and data.
  • Civic tech initiatives: Leveraging technology for transparent, participatory governance.
  • Community outreach: Collaborating with local organizations to address social issues.
  • Information policy: Advocating for open data, transparency, and digital rights.
  • Public-private partnerships: Forging collaborations to support community development.

Personal passions:

  • Lifelong learning: Pursuing continuing education and professional development.
  • Community building: Fostering inclusive, vibrant community spaces.
  • Social equity: Promoting fairness, justice, and equality in all aspects of life.
  • Technology innovation: Exploring emerging tech for social impact.
  • Leadership: Inspiring, motivating, and empowering others.

Career paths:

  • Director of Library Services
  • City/County Manager
  • Policy Analyst
  • Community Development Officer
  • Information Technology Director

Projects

  • Project 1: Designing a Mobile App for a Local Bank

    I worked with a team to design a mobile app for a local bank in Zambia. The app allows users to check their account balances, transfer money, and pay bills. I was responsible for creating the wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs for the app.

  • Project 2: Creating a Website for a Non-Profit Organization

    I designed a website for a non-profit organization that provides education and healthcare services to underprivileged children in Zambia. The website features a donation system, news section, and volunteer sign-up form. I was responsible for designing the layout, color scheme, and typography for the website.
